Leaving Earth 5 - medium solo

Jun 2 2017

Some solo goodness, a Leaving Earth medium difficulty scenario. I'm going to play this one out while getting other chores done. I used to do that a lot and it worked.

Here are the missions:

  1. Medium: Venus survey for 6.
  2. Medium: Mercury Survey for for 7.
  3. Easy: Man to Earth orbit and back for 4.
  4. Easy: Reveal Mars location for 5.
  5. Easy: Man to space and back for 2.
  6. Easy: Working Probe/Capsule in space for 1.

I'm definitely going for the Mercury Survey, and using an Ion Thruster to get there.

1956 Purchase Saturn Advancement and Saturn.

1957 Purchase Saturn, Soyuz Advancement.

1958 Purchase Probe, Surveying Advancement, Ion Thruster Advancement, 2 Probes, Vostok Capsule.

1959 Purchase 2 Soyuz, Atlas.

1960 Fire Saturn with Soyuz and Probe to Lunar Survey, this is just to get some cards turned. Saturn is success, Soyuz is success, this gets Probe into Lunar Orbit, now to Surveyâ€¦ Success. Too bad there is no mission for Lunar Survey. Microbes found. I have $5 left over, what to spend it on? Buy another Altas.

1961 Launch Saturn (Success) with Ion Thruster and Probe. Ion Thruster is a Minor Failure, oops. Purchase another Ion Thruster.

1962 Launch Saturn (Success, Advancement is clear) with Ion Thruster and Probe. Major Failure! Oh noes! Buy off the Failure and purchase 2 more Ion Thrusters. Those extra Probes I picked up are coming in handy now.

1963 Purchase Rendezvous and two Probes and another Ion Thruster.

1964 Purchase Saturn, launch two Ion/Probes. Rendezvous attempt to split spacecraft, Success. Minor failure on Ion Drive, scrapping the Venus mission. This is 3 failures on Ion Thrusters. Dang. (Mistake: I could have brought the Ion Thruster back to Earth for repair.)

1965 Purchase Saturn and Ion Thruster, and lets hope docking works. Major Failure for Rendezvous! Crap! That's $47 tied up and useless. Should have tested Rendezvous. I don't have any cash to remove the Major Failure, it's going back into the Outcomes. Bummer. (As above, I could have brought the damaged component back to Earth for repair.)

1966 I need to test and clear Rendezvous before going any further. In fact, because it's necessary for separation, I think it's something I need to start testing much earlier in the game. For now I'm going with the "speed bump" variant of Rendezvous, just using a single outcome. I launched a Saturn with 2 Junos, Rendezvous successful. Purchase Saturn and Ion Thruster. 1 year remaining on Mars probe. (Mistake here, cannot purchase components without the corresponding advancement if such exists.)

1967 Purchase Ion Thruster & Probes, launch, separate for Venus and Mercury. Put on trajectory for Inner Planets Transfer, both for 1 year trip, place Time Markers. Mars probe arrives next year. Purchase Soyuz and Apollo Capsule.

1968 Mars Probe arrives, seasonal growths of plant life, supplies available. Purchase Reentry Advancement. Two Soyuz stages with a Vostok capsule to test Soyuz and Reentry. Purchase Astronaut, Vladimir. Probes in Inner Planet Transfer, continue to Venus and Mercury Fly-Bys, respectively.

1969 Vostok Reentry, Success. Purchase Saturn. Remove Time Markers from Venus and Mars Fly Bys. Nothing at Mercury, Venus with Extreme Heat and Pressure, would have destroyed a lander. Purchase Vostok and launch both. Vostok Reentry for Major Failure, purchase for 5. Apollo stays in Earth Orbit.

1970 Apollo Capsule destroyed on Reentry, purchased Major Failure for 5.

Nothing else I really want to do, I'm calling the game here.

AAR

I'm taking the score as 18 from the 3 survey successes.

What to play next?

Deciding what to play next is always a tough call when faced with a closet full of games, all of which were purchased with intent to play. Here's one I'm dealing with the decision.

  • Something New: this means both something new to me and a game which has been published recently, within the last two or three years.
  • Something Classic: I've acquired a number of games properly considered "classic" which haven't yet been played. The current example would be PanzerGruppe Guderian.
  • Something Again: mastery requires constant practice, which means playing games many, many times to allow nuance to appear.
  • Something Vietnam: as I'm on a Vietnam binge. I don't have all the Vietnam games ever published, but I can say that I have a lot of them, and probably more than most people.
  • Something Obscure: as it's fun to poke around in the dustier corners of game design.
  • Something Different: break away from the same old publishers and try a game from one of the new kids on the block. Or, conversely, dip into legacy with a classic tried and true.
  • Something Light: once in a while it's nice to actually finish a game all the way through in one sitting, in an hour or two.
  • Something Heavy: if possible, it's nice to set up something big and complicated which can be left out for days or weeks or even months, for a leisurely stroll through a very large or long game.

There are surely many more ways to choose, but the above method keeps me busy enough, helps me get both breadth and depth into my play, and provides me with a sense of progress.

Upcoming Fall 2015

Here's a load of upcoming plays for Fall 2015. These are all scheduled for solo plays to learn rules and scenarios.

  • Brian Train's Green Beret. This is a two person game, which I'm going to solo a few turns on to get the rules figured out. It has hidden information which makes it fairly tedious to solo effectively.
  • Downtown, GMT's game on the air war in the Vietnam War. Has a great reputation. My plan is to play the first couple of scenarios to learn the rules. I have an opponent lined up for future games. Really looking forward to this one.
  • GDW's Suez '73, The Battle of Chinese Farm. Purported to be a classic. It has a number of scenarios, the first of which is a 1 turn effort which is what I'll play on this round. The campaign goes 10-12 turns. Update: Tonkin subsituted for this as I have found an opponent for Tonkin, and Suez '73 turns out to be much more involved than I imagined.
  • Karelia '44, an SCS game on the last(?) battle of the Finnish-Russian Continuation War. I recall it's supposed to be pretty good. Finns concede, hurt by a massive blunder allowing artillery to be overrun on a retreat back to the VTL.
  • Case Blue Kharkov 1942 scenario, 6 turns. This is doable even for me.
  • Operation Dynamo from France '40. I've played Sickle Cut solo and opposed. Allied sudden death win, enabled by French forces slipping off the south edge of the map.
  • Hapsburg Eclipse, a 4th play. I like these States of Siege games once in a while. They usually play pretty quickly as the game system hammers me. I've found the best way to win is to sit down for a "short, fast game."

This is probably a good two months worth of play, maybe 3. I'll be playing a fair number of other games with opponents along the way.
