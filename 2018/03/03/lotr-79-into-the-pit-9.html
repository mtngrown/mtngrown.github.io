We'll give it another try for today, after suffering three sudden death games in a row. Another play of my first dedicated dwarf deck.

Initial hand:

  1. Unexpected Courage
  2. Untrouble by Darkness
  3. A Test of Will
  4. Feint
  5. Untrouble by Darkness
  6. The Galadhrim's Greetings

Not a super great hand, but let's see how it plays out.

Initial encounter: Goblin Swordsman.

Round 1

  1. Resource: 3, draw Erebor Record Keeper.
  2. Planning: none.
  3. Quest: none, draw another Goblin Swordsman. Threat 31.
  4. Travel: none.
  5. Encounter: Both Goblin Swordsman.
  6. Combat: Dain and Thalin defend, shadow Watchful eyes on Dain, no effect and no damage, Lightless Passage on Dwalin, which cancels all damage, but no big deal, I can handle it so far.
  7. Refresh: Threat 32.

Round 2

  1. Resource: 6, draw The Galadhrim's Greetings.
  2. Planning: Unexpected Courage on Dwalin.
  3. Quest: none, draw Goblin Scout for Threat 35.
  4. Travel: none.
  5. Encounter: none.
  6. Combat: Feint on one of the Goblin Scouts, shadow for that is Signs of Conflict. Thalin defends other, shadow Crumbling Ruin for no effect, 1 D on Thalin. With Dain still up, Dwalin swings for 3 and kills Goblin scout, Threat 33.
  7. Refresh: Threat 34.

Round 3

  1. Resource: 6, draw Gandalf.
  2. Planning: none.
  3. Quest: none, draw Dark and Dreadful, no effect since no questing.
  4. Travel: none.
  5. Encounter: none.
  6. Combat: Thalin defends, draw shadow Lightless Passage which cancels damage. Bummer, can't clear this fucker.
  7. Refresh: Threat 35.

Round 4

  1. Resource: 9, draw Sneak Attack.
  2. Planning: none.
  3. Quest: Thalin, draw Branching Paths, net 0.
  4. Travel: none.
  5. Encounter: none.
  6. Combat: Dain defends, Goblin Swordsman, add to the Staging Area. No damage on Dain, Dwalin kills for Threat 34.
  7. Refresh: Threat 35.

Round 5

  1. Resource: 12, draw Erebor Record Keeper.
  2. Planning: none.
  3. Quest: Thalin, draw Zigil Mineshaft. Play Gandalf Sneak Attack to quest for +4 more and, Threat 38.
  4. Travel: none.
  5. Encounter: Goblin Swordsman.
  6. Combat: Dain defends, draw Stairs of Nain and exhaust Dwalin no damage to Dain, Dwalin readies and defends, draw Goblin Follower with +5 Attack which kills Dwalin, and concede.
  7. Refresh: Threat

This deck is really hard to get started.

Also, I have not been exploiting Zigil Mineshaft, for which I could be raising Threat to explore it. I need to start doing that in the future. I may bring Citadel Plate back in.

The hardest thing is figuring out how to get through the East-Gate within the first two rounds. It requires 7 quest, and the best I can get is 4 on the first turn.

What to play next?

Deciding what to play next is always a tough call when faced with a closet full of games, all of which were purchased with intent to play. Here's one I'm dealing with the decision.

  • Something New: this means both something new to me and a game which has been published recently, within the last two or three years.
  • Something Classic: I've acquired a number of games properly considered "classic" which haven't yet been played. The current example would be PanzerGruppe Guderian.
  • Something Again: mastery requires constant practice, which means playing games many, many times to allow nuance to appear.
  • Something Vietnam: as I'm on a Vietnam binge. I don't have all the Vietnam games ever published, but I can say that I have a lot of them, and probably more than most people.
  • Something Obscure: as it's fun to poke around in the dustier corners of game design.
  • Something Different: break away from the same old publishers and try a game from one of the new kids on the block. Or, conversely, dip into legacy with a classic tried and true.
  • Something Light: once in a while it's nice to actually finish a game all the way through in one sitting, in an hour or two.
  • Something Heavy: if possible, it's nice to set up something big and complicated which can be left out for days or weeks or even months, for a leisurely stroll through a very large or long game.

There are surely many more ways to choose, but the above method keeps me busy enough, helps me get both breadth and depth into my play, and provides me with a sense of progress.

  • Have a solo game ready to play.
  • Keep score or advise on a game in progress.
